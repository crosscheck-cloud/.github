# Security Policy

## Reporting a vulnerability

If you discover a security issue in any Crosscheck product or repository, please report it privately to:

**info@crosscheck.cloud**

Please do not open a public issue, pull request, or discussion thread for security matters. Public disclosure before a fix is shipped puts users at risk.

## What to include in your report

To help us triage quickly, please include as much of the following as you can:

- A clear description of the issue
- Steps to reproduce, or a proof-of-concept
- The product, repository, or URL affected
- Browser, operating system, and extension version (if applicable)
- The potential impact as you see it
- Your name or handle for credit (optional)

## What to expect from us

- We will acknowledge your report within **72 hours**.
- We will provide a detailed response within **7 business days** with our assessment and an estimated timeline.
- We will keep you informed as we work on a fix.
- Once the issue is resolved, we will publicly credit you in the release notes — unless you prefer to remain anonymous.

## Scope

This policy covers:

- The Crosscheck Chrome extension
- The Crosscheck web app at `app.crosscheck.cloud`
- The Crosscheck marketing site at `crosscheck.cloud`
- The Crosscheck backend API at `api.crosscheck.cloud`
- The Crosscheck MCP server at `mcp.crosscheck.cloud`
- The Crosscheck CDN at `cdn.crosscheck.cloud`
- Public repositories under the Crosscheck GitHub organization

## Out of scope

- Findings that require physical access to a user's device
- Issues in third-party services we depend on (please report those upstream)
- Theoretical issues without a working proof-of-concept
- Social engineering of Crosscheck staff
- Denial of service via volumetric attack

## Safe harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Make a good-faith effort to avoid privacy violations and service disruption
- Only test against accounts they own or have explicit permission to test
- Do not exfiltrate, modify, or destroy data beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability
- Give us a reasonable time to respond before any public disclosure

Thank you for helping keep Crosscheck and its users safe.
